Your protocol

Three things move on different clocks: water (days), gut bloat (hours), fat (weeks). Over 3 weeks, water and gut do most of the visible work. Protein + heavy loads keep the muscle.

Daily targets @ ~80 kg

  • ~2,250 kcal — a moderate ~20% deficit, not a crash.
  • ~190 g protein (2.4 g/kg) across 3–4 feedings of 40–50 g. The single biggest muscle-sparing lever.
  • ~65–70 g fat — hormone floor, don't go under.
  • ~215 g carbs, parked around training to fuel heavy lifting.

De-bloat layer

  • Water 3–3.5 L, steady & high — downregulates the hormone that makes you hold water. Drink more, not less.
  • Sodium moderate & constant — crashing it spikes aldosterone and backfires.
  • Potassium ~3,500–4,000 mg (greens, potatoes, fruit) — the Na:K ratio is what flattens subcutaneous water.
  • Carbs: each gram of glycogen holds ~3 g water. A modest trim smooths you out; don't zero them.

Training — non-negotiable

  • Keep your 5-day split heavy, same loads. Load is the signal that says "keep the muscle."
  • If fatigue stacks by week 2, drop a set or two — not the weight.
  • 7,500+ steps / easy Z2 walks for the deficit. No hard cardio competing with lifting recovery.
  • Sleep 7–9 h. One bad week can mask all your fat loss under cortisol water.

What to expect

  • Scale may drop 2–4 kg, but a big chunk of week 1 is water + gut emptying — that's the "deflate."
  • Actual fat is maybe 1.5–2.5 kg of it. Muscle stays if protein and loads stay.
  • Don't panic at fast week-1 loss or a week-2 stall — water rebounds and hides fat loss constantly.
  • Judge by the mirror, the 3-day average, and the bar — not daily scale noise.

Worth a check

  • All-day constant puffiness that never shifts — especially if it pits when pressed, or sits in ankles/face — deserves a doctor's once-over (renal / cardiac / thyroid) before assuming it's nutrition. Food-driven water responds within days; that's your tell.
